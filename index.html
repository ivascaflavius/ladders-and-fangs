How to Play

No one to play with? Pick Play vs Computer from the main menu — the AI opponent rolls, chooses moves, and plays cards to try to win, so you can play solo any time.

Each player controls 2 tokens, both starting off-board. Roll a die, then choose which token to move — that choice is the game.

A token can't overshoot square 100; it must land exactly on it.

Land exactly on an opponent's token to send it back to start — unless that square is safe (ladder bottoms, snake heads, and square 1).

You can't stack your own two tokens on one square.

Land on a card square to draw a power-up (max 2 held):

  • Shield — automatic/reactive. You don't tap it — it kicks in on its own the moment one of your tokens would be bitten by a snake, keeping the token in place instead of sliding down.
  • Swap — tap it on your turn instead of rolling; pick one of your tokens and one opponent token to swap positions.
  • Double Move — after you roll, it appears as an extra option next to your token choices; pick it to move both your tokens by the roll instead of just one. If one token is already locked in at 100, it instead sends your other token twice as far (roll × 2).
  • Trap — tap it on your turn instead of rolling, then tap an empty, ordinary square on the board to rig it. If your opponent's token lands there, a die is rolled and the token is knocked back that many squares ×5 (so 5-30 squares) — Shield won't save them, it only blocks snakes. Traps stay hidden: you'll see your own on the board, but never your opponent's, until one springs.

A token that reaches square 100 is locked in — it can't be captured or swapped away. First player to get both tokens to exactly square 100 wins!
